📋 The Excel Bid Workbook — The Core Bottleneck WHAT WE'RE AUTOMATING
What it is

The Excel bid workbook is the single artifact that captures every scope item for a refrigeration project — equipment counts, pipe quantities, fittings, labor hours, and material pricing. It is built entirely by hand from drawing review, using vendor price grids (like United Refrigeration's job estimating sheets) to look up costs line by line.

How it reaches FieldJetX

Once the workbook is complete, the Excel file is uploaded directly into FieldJetX for bid tracking and project management. The upload works — but the workbook itself still had to be built entirely by hand first, line by line, from drawing review. Every error made during that manual build goes straight into FieldJetX with it.

What Zeus changes

Zeus reads the drawings, extracts quantities using AI, applies pricing deterministically, and auto-populates the Excel workbook. The estimator reviews and approves — then one click exports it directly into FieldJetX. No manual re-entry. No double-handling. No silent errors.

Root Causes

Six Recurring Failure Modes

A qualitative review of bid-review emails from senior estimators identified six patterns that account for the vast majority of costly bid errors. These aren't one-off mistakes — they recur on nearly every project.

Failure Mode 01
📐

Spec-to-Material Mismatches

Pipe type, pressure rating, insulation thickness, condensate drain material, and furnished valves repeatedly diverge between specs and what's actually entered in the bid workbook. One undetected spec requirement cascades into multiple wrong workbook inputs.

High Frequency · High Cost
Failure Mode 02
📅

Stale & Volatile Pricing

Bids often rely on pricing grids that haven't been updated to reflect recent commodity changes or vendor quote revisions. The system has no date-awareness and cannot warn when material costs have drifted since the last price pull — a silent, expensive mistake.

Direct Margin Impact
Failure Mode 03
🗂️

Missing Secondary Material Categories

Case drains, PVC, Portapacks, oil/filter/drier changes, condensers, demo labor, jacketing, and customer-specific hardware are easy omissions because they sit outside the main pipe-and-labor takeoff flow. No checklist catches them systematically.

Scope Exposure
Failure Mode 04
🔢

Quantity Reconciliation Gaps

Bid workbook inputs are rarely cross-checked against equipment schedules or RS drawings. Case counts, evaporator counts, valve counts, and piping quantities drift between documents with no automated flag — leaving reviewers to find discrepancies by eye.

High Detection Difficulty
Failure Mode 05
⚙️

Indirect & Job-Condition Assumptions

HFG factors are used as a catch-all risk bucket. Fittings, clamps, ½" hardware, and XHP-related costs are hidden in a percentage rather than explicitly priced. After-hours work, phasing restrictions, night-work rules, and temporary refrigeration requirements are assumed, not verified.

Hidden Risk
Failure Mode 06
📝

Workbook & Version-Control Issues

Multiple estimators work off different spreadsheet versions with no audit trail. When a reviewer finds a problem, there's no systematic record of what assumption changed, who changed it, and what the financial impact was — making continuous improvement impossible.

Operational Risk
$750K+

The Hidden Cost of Undetected Errors

Any single estimate, alternate, change-order opportunity, or risk exposure above $750,000 currently has no automatic flag. Pricing drift on major materials, missed XHP/fitting exposure, and overlooked scope categories have been the most financially impactful errors — discovered only during post-award review or at project closeout.

Proposed Solution

Extending Zeus into a Multi-Layer AI Estimating Platform

Zeus is already ClimatePros' own on-premises AI — running Claude Sonnet 4.6 on the Mac Studio "Tundra" via OpenClaw, currently serving the executive team for FieldJetX queries and operational reporting. The proposal is to extend Zeus as the primary extraction layer in a multi-model AI stack that automates the entire bid workbook — with independent verifier models adding a systematic safety net that no single model can provide alone.

Architecture Principle

"LLMs do quantities. Code does dollars."

All three AI models extract scope and quantities only. Every price is computed by pure, auditable, deterministic code. No model ever emits a dollar figure — ensuring 100% price auditability and zero hallucinated costs.

📥

Multi-Format Intake

Accepts PDF, Word, CAD, scans, and photos. Normalizes to clean pages; extracts text natively or via Zeus vision — no manual re-entry required.
